Allama Iqbal

**Life:** Allama Muhammad Iqbal was born on 9 November 1877 in Sialkot. He received his early education in his hometown and later studied philosophy at Government College, Lahore. For higher studies, he went to Europe where he earned degrees from Cambridge University, Lincoln’s Inn (Law), and a PhD in Philosophy from the University of Munich, Germany. He passed away on 21 April 1938 in Lahore.

**Achievements:** Iqbal was not only a great poet but also a philosopher, thinker, and lawyer. He wrote inspiring poetry in Urdu and Persian, which earned him the title *“Poet of the East.”* His famous works include *Bang-e-Dra*, *Asrar-e-Khudi*, and *Bal-e-Jibril*. Through his writings, he revived the spirit of Muslims and gave them confidence and hope. His famous Allahabad Address in 1930 presented the idea of a separate homeland for Muslims.

**Contributions:** Allama Iqbal awakened the Muslims of the subcontinent by teaching them self-respect, unity, and the importance of knowledge. He inspired the younger generation to build strength through hard work and faith. Politically, he is remembered as the spiritual father of Pakistan because his vision laid the foundation for the creation of the country. His ideas continue to motivate people even today.

About Allama Iqbal

Allama Iqbal’s vision centered on awakening selfhood (*khudi*) and empowering individuals to realize their inner strength. He emphasized unity, faith, and self-reliance as the foundation for Muslim revival. His message inspired the dream of a free homeland where Muslims could live with dignity and purpose.
